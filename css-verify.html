What is Volume Assessment?

When a patient is in shock or has borderline urine output, we need to decide: give more fluid, start pressors, or diurese?

Question 1
Is this patient fluid responsive?
Will giving fluid actually increase cardiac output?
Question 2
Is this patient fluid tolerant?
Can we give fluid safely, without causing harm or organ congestion?
💡 Key Insight

These are two independent questions. A patient can be fluid responsive but NOT fluid tolerant — or vice versa.

IVC vs VExUS

📏

Answers →
Fluid Responsiveness

Use IVC + VExUS together to make the final decision

A patient is fluid responsive. What does this mean you should do?
✅ Correct. Fluid responsiveness alone does not mean you should give fluid. You must also determine whether the patient can tolerate it — using VExUS or other congestion markers.
✅ Summary

Module 1 Key Takeaways

💧
Fluid Responsiveness
  • Heart is preload-dependent
  • Bolus raises cardiac output ≥10–15%
  • Assessed by IVC collapsibility or PLR
🛡️
Fluid Tolerance
  • No harmful venous congestion present
  • Organs can safely receive more volume
  • Assessed by VExUS grading
Always answer both questions before acting —
fluid responsiveness and fluid tolerance.
